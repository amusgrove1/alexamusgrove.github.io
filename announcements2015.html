Now accepting Board of Directors applications

10/3/2015

If you are interested in running for a seat on the PF HOA Board of Directors, you can download a application form here.

The day to day operations of the PF HOA are run by an all-volunteer Board of Directors, along with help from many other committee volunteers. The Board establishes the annual dues assessment and is responsible for using the money assessed to maintain and improve HOA property and offer programs which are of value to Prairie Fields residents, thereby increasing the value of the neighborhood. If you have 1-2 hours a month to donate to the neighborhood, you are encouraged to run one of the 3 upcoming vacancies on the Board for a term which begins January 1, 2016 and runs through December 31, 2018. Applications must be submitted by October 15.

This year, voting will be conducted electronically. You can opt for paper ballots by dropping a note in the HOA dropbox. Homeowners must be a member in good standing to vote in the election, and not owe the association more than $10. You can access thepast due list by clicking here.

For more information, please emailboard@prairiefieldshoa.com

Read this before you do exterior home improvement

3/13/2015

Now the spring is around the corner and families may be thinking about a new deck, shed, fence or other structure, you are reminded that the PFHOA Covenant requires that your plan or proposal must be submitted to the Architectural Control Committee (ACC), who review and ensure that your request follows the covenants that are required for your section.

As you may know, Prairie Fields has 14 sets of covenants that must be complied with, and its the responsibility of the ACC verify your request follows the covenants. Most covenants address issues related to location on the property, materials used, colors (prairie earth tone colors), building permits, etc. The ACC request form is available on the PFHOA website. Please let ACC know if you have any questions or simply submit your plan on the website

223 of your neighbors have connected on Nextdoor.com

2/12/2015

Many of your neighbors in Prairie Fields use Nextdoor.com to share information including events, road closures, lost pets, and babysitting services.

Nextdoor.com is a free social network the PFHOA is using to help the neighbors of Prairie Fields connect and communicate online about important information, services, and goings-on in their community.

Members choose how much information they want to disclose on their personal profiles. Once residents verify their addresses and sign up for the site using their real names, they have access to a neighborhood directory, map, events, marketplace ("buy," "sell," and "free") and local service recommendations like "great babysitter" or nearby resources such as veterinarians, dentists, schools, country clubs, and churches.
